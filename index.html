An example of how the rules make everything better: Kpop

Kpop isn't for fun. It's a business. It's not just 5, 6, 7, 8 — it's 10. Always.

Collectivist culture combined with a tacit agreement to 'the rules' creates an enjoyable climate of content that thrives on structure.

1
The training system ensures participants ("idols") meet set standards — you can trust they'll be qualified for the job.
2
Groups are built in planned structures — deliberate roles and responsibilities means expectations are clear for both participants and viewers.
3
We all know what success looks like — agreed scoring systems mean clear and equal markers for success and achievement.
4
Merit is awarded by discipline — hard work is part of the job.

The result — well, it's not all bad. With a foundation of discipline, clear responsibilities and defined rules and roles, excellence becomes the standard, and everything above it an extension of that. Check below for my favourite kpop bops.

The cup noodle museum (Yokohama, Japan)
🍜
One of my greatest life experiences.
Finally.

What's it about?

Momofuku Ando, the inventor of cup noodles. But it's more than that…

The museum tells his story — and the story of other inventors, showing how:

His diligence in solving a problem created the cup noodles we know and love.
The noodles were created with five objectives, which we still see now: taste, non-perishable, quick, cheap, sanitary.
He turned things upside down — literally — and created the opportunity for mass production.
He ensured justice by founding the Instant Food Industry Association to set fair competition and standards.
The museum doesn't just demonstrate the importance of the invention but that big problems can be solved with simple solutions — as long as you have the diligence to find it.
